There’s no natural way to move the breast closer together. This phenomenon is usually related to the shape of the underlying boney chest wall. Correction would be difficult and associated with a high failure rate because the chest wall would remain unchanged. In most cases, the use of a good support bra would be the best solution. Moving breasts closer together

The short answer is that there is no non-surgical way to move breasts closer together. There is not even a good surgical way to do it for cosmetic purposes. Fortunately your breasts are not far apart and have a normal gap between them.

They only look far apart when not upright and when there is nothing filling out the inner (medial) edge of your breast which you can see when you push them together. To fit and look right, a breast implant must fit to the true width of your breast and would give you steeper cleavage but not move them closer together.

The minimum volume or forward profile implant is called a low profile in saline-filled (Allergan only) but not called that in gels even though it’s the equivalent of a low profile (Mentor or Allergan). This would make your breasts look closer together and with a steeper cleavage but not actually move them. The size increase would be about a cup size.

If you cannot accept an implant and the one cup size increase, the only option I can think of would be fat grafting the medial edge of your breast. This is not yet considered standard treatment and has not been proven to have no effect on breast cancer in young women for cosmetic purposes. Therefore I cannot recommend it. A minimal forward volume implant of the correct width and positioning is the simplest solution. (Scott L. Replogle, MD, Denver Plastic Surgeon)

You may need a mastopexy or breast lift to reposition the breasts. Breast implants may also be required to improve the amount of cleavage that you have. (Jeffrey E. Schreiber, MD, FACS, Baltimore Plastic Surgeon)
