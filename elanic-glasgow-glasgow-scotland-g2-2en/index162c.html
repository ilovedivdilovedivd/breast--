Richard Clark, MD, MRCS, FRCS(Plast)

Name: Richard Clark, MD, MRCS, FRCS(Plast)
Statement: Richard specialises in cosmetic surgery, breast surgery and skin cancer surgery. Which he delivers both to NHS Forth Valley, BMI Kings Park Stirling, Glasgow Day Surgery Centre, and now to The Academy of Plastic Surgery.

For the previous 18 years (since 2003) his dedication has seen him complete a fellowship within Head and Neck Surgery. Gaining skills to perform surgery on the face, eyelids, ears, and nose. A further fellowship studying all aspects of skin cancer. This includes Mohs’ surgery which can also be added to Richards repertoire of expert surgical interventions.

Most recently, Richard’s skills have seen him carrying out breast reshaping and reconstruction. As a result, Mr Clark can be found on the GMC Specialist Register as a Plastic Surgeon.

A little background information as you get to know Richard
‍Richard attended Glasgow University and graduated in 1998. Following the necessary basic surgical training he then began his career in plastic surgery. Most of this being undertaken at Canniesburn NHS Hospital in Glasgow.

Canniesburn Hospital, prior to it’s move to Glasgow Royal, was the hub of the West of Scotland Regional service and all major plastic surgery was centralised there. A training place like no other.

By 2004, Richard began a research degree in skin cancer. This led to the MD Research Degree Award. Further supported by the publication of several scientific papers.

In 2007. Richard was appointed to the Pan-Scotland Plastic Surgery training scheme. As an active member he spent time in Dundee, Belgium, Detroit, and Norwich developing specialist skills in breast surgery, cosmetic surgery, and skin cancer surgery.

His continual rise to success found him awarded the highly sought-after Training Interface group fellowships in 2013.

NHS
In 2014, Richard undertook the position of Consultant Plastic Surgeon within NHS Forth Valley. He is responsible for breast reconstruction surgery. A service for which he was instrumental in developing and setting up. His NHS consultant role also see him carrying out skin cancer surgeries. He is one of very few plastic surgeons trained in oncoplastic breast surgery. Integral to all breast cancer surgeries. Greatly enhancing the range of options that can be offered to women with breast cancer and facilitates better outcomes from it.

Furthermore, he is one of very few surgeons whose skills extend to Mohs’ surgery. A microscopically controlled surgery used to treat common types of skin cancer.
