Risk for Sculptra with Keloid Conditions?
Sculptra & Keloids
Feb 13, 2013
The manufacturer of Sculptra recommends that the product not be used on individuals with a history of keloids. Sculptra works by stimulating collagen and the source of keloids is excessive collagen stimulation. It is possible that you could develop lumpiness in the areas treated.
Local Anesthesia (Tumescent Technique) & IV Sedation for Breast Augmentation?
Anesthesia
Feb 1, 2013
After 15 years of experience with local anesthesia with IV sedation for breast augmentation this is my preferred method. Patients are very comfortable, experience quick recovery and have had no anesthetic complications. I see no need to use general anesthesia, although some physicians do choose this.
Do You Think Tricare Prime Will Cover a Breast Reduction for Me?
Each Insurance Provider is Different
Jan 31, 2013
Each insurance company has different criteria for determining whether they will cover breast reduction surgery. You need to have a consultation with a plastic surgeon who will gather information such as your height, weight, breast measurement, photos and whether you have a history of neck and back pain. The doctor will then submit that information to your insurance provider for pre-authorization. If approved, your surgeon can schedule you for the procedure. Best of luck to you!
What Does Tough Fat Have to Do with the Results of Triplex Laser Lipo?
“Tough Fat”
Jan 15, 2013
In my experience, many young males your age have “fibrous” tissue, particularly in the flank areas, which takes more time and laser energy to treat. Assuming that your surgeon used the appropriate laser settings, you should see good results by about three months.
Which is my Best Option Fillers or Lower Bleph?
Puffy Eyes
Jan 15, 2013
I would recommend that you consider a transconjunctival blepharoplasty for the lower lids to reduce the volume of the three fat pads in that area. The addition of a filler such as Belotero along the infraorbital rim would also enhance the result.
Afraid 475cc May Be Too Big?
Implants too large?
Jan 15, 2013
Given your height and weight a 475cc implant seems a bit large in my opinion. If you have done the rice test and feel nervous about the size you should consult your plastic surgeon prior to your surgery date. In my experience, a 300-350cc implant is a much more common size choice. I believe a 475cc implant will make you a large D cup.
Replacement of Saline Breast Implants?
Replacing implants
Sep 25, 2012
Since you liked the result that you had for 20 years, I see no reason to change the implant position. A silicone gel implant above the muscle would likely look and feel even more natural than the saline did. Choose an ASPS plastic surgeon and make the choice together.
