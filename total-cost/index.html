Breast Enhancement Total Cost

Breast enlargement, breast augmentation, breast implants, financing, cost

The total cost of breast augmentation (enlargement) varies between physicians, regions of the country and where the surgery takes place. Typically, costs for breast augmentation run between $4,000 and $7,000 for saline implants and $5,000 to $8,000 for silicone gels. Patients should be sure to inquire about any additional (or hidden) costs when they consult with a physician.

For instance, a surgeon who does not operate his or her own surgical center may require patients to pay fees to the hospital or surgical center they will be using separately from the fees paid to the surgeon. (Donna Rich, MD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

Breast aug pricing

Pricing can be very confusing since some surgeons quote the whole cost as a flat charge and others break it down in parts. In the end, you pay for

  1. The surgeon’s time in the procedure,
  2. The operating room costs and cost of anesthesia,
  3. Implants,
  4. Blood work and clearance possibly needed,
  5. Prescriptions, bras, and scar creams, etc used after.

I have seen surgery costs as low as 2500 and as high as 8500 in my area. Some of the factors that make surgery more expensive are.

1. Silicone implants are more expensive than saline (by up to $1000). Form stable implants/gummy bear implants can be up to $1000 more expensive than silicone. Some surgeons get better pricing than others, but if your surgeon’s implants are far less expensive than everyone else’s, you have to question why.

2. Going to a hospital instead of surgery center or office. Hospitals are most expensive. Offices are generally least expensive, but be wary if it’s too cheap. Some surgeons don’t maintain proper safety standards. You are looking for a facility with active accreditation by state and national organizations. Don’t be afraid to call the accreditation association and make sure the facility is still certified.

3. Using sizers. I use silicone sizers to try implants on during surgery. Not only does this take extra time, but there is also a cost for the sizers. Surgeons who don’t do this can still get great results, but I like making sure the implants look as good as possible before selecting a final size. (Dana Goldberg, MD, Jupiter Plastic Surgeon)
