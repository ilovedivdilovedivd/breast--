Breast Augmentation Costs

When considering the cost of breast augmentation, consider the following rough cost estimates:

  1. Surgeon’s fees (may vary from $2000 – $5000)
  2. Implant fees (may vary from $1000 – $2000) depending on saline or silicone.
  3. Facility costs (may vary from $1000 – $2000)
  4. Anesthesia costs (may vary from $500 – $1500)

This should be give you the range of costs for a routine breast augmentation ($4500 – $10500).

Most estimates will be in somewhere in the middle as the range of fees are all set independently unless your surgeon has their own facility.

I work at a hospital with very reasonable facility and anesthesia fees so my costs tend to be on the lower side of the range. (Young R. Cho, MD, PhD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

The fees for elective cosmetic procedures vary from city to city. It is very important to compare apples to apples though, make sure your price quotes are from only BOARD CERTIFIED PLASTIC SURGEONS. Not Cosmetic surgeons pretending to be plastic surgeons. You will want to know the following: How many BAM’s do you perform a year? Do you have privileges to perform this procedure in the hospital? Where is this procedure performed? What is the total cost – surgeon’s fee, facility fee, implant fee, anesthesia fee, prescriptions and meds, garments, post op care, photos, revision fee (if needed) What type of warranty do I get on the implants and from the surgeon? Do you offer financing and what are the terms of financing? The average cost for silicone implants is approximately $1,000 more than saline. My total cost fees are $4,700 for saline and $5,700 for silicone. (David Finkle, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Breast augmentation surgery cost should not be first factor

Although cost is a crucial part of making a decision to have breast augmentation surgery, it should not be the first. As you see from the answers already given the cost can vary dramatically. Beware of the lowest bidder or if someone says “I will beat any price”. You are not buying a TV. You are buying expertise, care and long term outcome. First choose a surgeon by their credentials – board certification, local respected hospital plastic surgery privileges, then by referrals – other doctors, patients, etc. Once you have narrowed down two well respected surgeons, then see if they match your personality and that you like their bedside manner.

Also make sure that the after surgery care is just as attentive as the preop. Speak with former patients if you need to. Once you have done all that homework usually one surgeon stands out and cost at that point does not matter. If for some reason you still can’t decide, then compare costs and make the best choice possible.

Some charge very low to get your business and some charge very high to appear better eventhough they are not – so fees are not a good place to start. They will not tell you anything about the surgeon or their outcome or their care. Word of mouth about their reputation is the best place to start. Fees are only one of many factors to consider. (Bahram Ghaderi, MD, Manhattan Plastic Surgeon)
