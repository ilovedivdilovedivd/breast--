Dana Khuthaila, MD, FRCSC, FACS

Name: Dana Khuthaila, MD, FRCSC, FACS
Statement: Dr. Dana Khuthaila, MD, FRCSC, FACS is a double board-certified plastic surgeon in New York City who’s been in practice for 17 years. She is an exceptionally talented and skilled cosmetic surgeon with specialties in cosmetic and reconstructive surgery for the breasts, body, and face.

After obtaining her medical degree from The College of Medicine at King Saudi University in Saudi Arabia, she completed a residency in plastic surgery at McMaster University in Ontario, Canada, and a fellowship in cosmetic surgery at the Cosmetic Surgery Hospital in Toronto, Canada.

To further hone her craft, Dr. Khuthaila pursued additional training across the globe. This postgraduate education included cosmetic surgery studies at Clinica Ivo Pitanguy in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil; breast cosmetics at the Center for Breast and Body Contouring in Grand Rapids, Michigan; breast microsurgery reconstruction at Ghent University Hospital in Belgium; breast conserving surgery at the European Institute of Oncology in Milan; and breast cosmetic surgery at Milan’s National Institute for the Study of Reconstructive Surgery. She also worked at the Boston Children’s Hospital, where she corrected cleft lips and palates.

Dr. Khuthaila is double board-certified by the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada - Plastic Surgery, and by the American Board of Plastic Surgery. She is a member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons (ASPS) and the International Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery (ISAPS), and is a fellow, American College of Surgeons (FACS).

Her years of education and experience has earned Dr. Khuthaila recognition and praise from both her patients and fellow plastic surgeons. Castle Connolly Top Doctors included her on the esteemed roster of Exceptional Women in Medicine.

Stitches or mesh for lateral displacement?
Lateral displacement of breast implants.
Jan 16, 2019
Thank you for the question. I use stitches to close the lateral pocket but only after I change the implant pocket to above the muscle. The reason for the lateral displacement is the continued contraction of the Pectoralis muscle pushing the implant down and to the side where there is no muscle. I have had success with this technique without the use of unnecessary mesh. Hope that helps. All the best,Dr. Khuthaila
