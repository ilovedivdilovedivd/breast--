Choosing A Plastic Surgeon For Breast Augmentation

How to find a best surgeons for breast augmentation

There are many ways to find a best surgeons for breast augmentation including personal resources such as friends and family as well as professional organizations.

When considering cosmetic surgery, knowing the credentials of the surgeon you are consulting with is essential.

I would advocate that for breast augmentation, a Plastic Surgeon who is board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery should be among your absolute criteria.

These doctors have completed years of rigorous training specifically focusing on plastic surgery, have been in the practice of plastic surgery for a minimum of 1 year, and have undergone several examinations (both oral and written) to determine their competency in all aspects of plastic surgery (including all aspects of breast reconstruction and cosmetic breast surgery).

Best surgeons for breast augmentation for best result

Only by passing all these hurdles can a surgeon be designated a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon. Resources online to identify a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon in your area include:

  • The American Board of Medical Specialties: www.abms.org
  • The American Society of Plastic Surgeons: www.plasticsurgery.org
  • The American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery: www.surgery.org (Scott Thellman, M.D., Lawrence Plastic Surgeon)

Do Your Research

We all want to choose the best doctor to take care of us or our family, so do your research with phone calls and the Internet, do not rush into surgery blindly. Here are the questions you will need to ask the doctors: Are your Board Certified in Plastic Surgery? Do you have privileges to perform this procedure in the hospital and what hospitals do you have privileges at? Where is the surgery performed? How many Breast Augmentations do you perform annually? Best qualified surgeon for breast augmentation

Choosing a qualified surgeon to perform your breast augmentation surgery is one of the most important decisions you can make. When checking the credentials of your surgeon, look for a surgeon who is board certified in plastic surgery thru the American Board of Plastic Surgery. Additionally, ask your surgeon if he or she is a member of ASPS, the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, as well as ASAPS, the American Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery. As a member of both of these societies, you can feel confident that your surgeon has met the strictest standards available in plastic surgery.

Additionally, when meeting with your prospective surgeon, ask how many breast augmentation surgeries are performed annually by that surgeon and ask to see before and after photos of patients that the surgeon has operated on. Family and friends are also a good source of information, many of whom may have heard about the surgeon or been a patient of the surgeon. Spending time researching your potential surgeon is an important aspect of surgical decision making and will be worth the effort, in the long run. (E. Ronald Finger, MD, Savannah Plastic Surgeon)
