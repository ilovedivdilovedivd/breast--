While your breasts do have a gap, you should still be able to get a nice result from breast augmentation. As long as you visit a board-certified plastic surgeon with experience in performing breast augmentations for women with wide-set breasts, you should not end up with breasts that look like the second photo. There are techniques that a skilled surgeon can use to help reduce the gap between your breasts, although the amount that this gap can be reduced depends on your anatomy.

You should be able to go back to school one week after a breast augmentation. As long as the recovery process goes smoothly and patients follow their post-op instructions, most are able to return to work or school within a week. However, you should not lift a heavy backpack or ride your bike after just one week.
