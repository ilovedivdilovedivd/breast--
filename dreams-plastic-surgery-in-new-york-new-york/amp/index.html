Which procedure would target sagging, volume loss, and areola size?
Start with a lift
Jan 7, 2023
I would first do a lift, to lift them and improve symmetry. Areaolas are automatically decreased in size during a lift. Then I would see how they look and put an implant in for more fullness if needed. Alternatively you can do both at once.
What’s the best option for sagging and side boob due to recent weight loss? 112 lbs down, goal is sma
two options
Jan 7, 2023
One is to do a lift with extension of the horizontal incision laterally, to cut out the extra skin on the side. Another option is to do a lift with agressive liposuction on the side. When the fat is removed, the skin will come in, getting rid of the side fullness without the scar there.
Surgeon didn’t mark me up before surgery – why?
if for breast augmentation, if he is a minimalist, only one marking can be used which can be done in the OR
Jan 7, 2023
For a breast lift, surgeons need a lot of markings. For implants, the only marking you really need is where you will make the incision, underneath the breast. That marking can be done in the operating room, while you are lying down he would push the breast downwards, so it reforms the crease, and puts a marking in there. I mark the breasts before hand, but if someone has been doing it for 30 years, and all they do is breast implants, maybe they are comfortable doing markings later.
Which procedure options would help correct deflated breasts with minimal scarring?
small implant
Jan 7, 2023
I would put a small implant to fill out the superior pole more and to give them more fullness. Since you dont want to be bigger, put a small implant, just to give the volume. The lift you dont need, it wount make much difference just give you a scar.
Asymmetrical breasts 1 year post op – What could have caused this?
were they symmetric before?
Jan 7, 2023
You have to look at your pre op photos, sometimes you will see some asymmetry there, even though you didnt notice it before because you were not looking close enough. They look in the right place and the same size. Perhaps one side is forming a capsular contracture, if it is becoming hard. I would talk to the surgeon who put them in.
