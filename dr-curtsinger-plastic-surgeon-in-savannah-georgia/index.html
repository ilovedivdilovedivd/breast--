Statement: Board-certified plastic surgeon Dr. Luke J. Curtsinger treats patients of all ages from across the state of Georgia, from Savannah and Bluffton to Brunswick and Hinesville. In over 28 years of practice, he has performed more than 20,000 surgeries, all with the goal of satisfying his patients. “Nothing makes me happier than when my patients look at me with tears in their eyes because, finally, they are happy with what they see,” he says.

Dr. Curtsinger specializes in many plastic surgery procedures, including breast enhancement (breast augmentations, breast lifts, and more); facelift surgery and neck lift surgery; mommy makeovers (including tummy tucks and Brazilian butt lifts); Botox, Dysport, cosmetic fillers, and more, giving patients in the Savannah area and beyond a plethora of options for looking and feeling their best. He performs all of his procedures at the AAAASF-accredited Savannah Plastic Surgery Surgicenter, a state-licensed ambulatory surgical facility that is a more comfortable and affordable option than a hospital setting.

After graduating magna cum laude from Murray State University, Dr. Curtsinger went on to receive his medical degree from the University of Louisville School of Medicine, where he earned multiple accolades and graduated in the top 20 percent of his class. After completing his general surgery residency at the University of Texas Health Science Center, he completed two fellowships—the first in wound healing at the University of Louisville and the second in plastic and reconstructive surgery at the University of Pittsburgh. He is board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery and is a member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons.

Dr. Curtsinger is extremely active in the medical community. He has been an assistant professor of plastic surgery at East Carolina University, the chief of plastic surgery and director of plastic surgery education at Memorial Health University Medical Center in Savannah, Georgia, and was the 2018 president of the Georgia Medical Society. He frequently lectures about skin healing, and has authored 15 scientific papers on this subject and others. Dr. Curtsinger also co-founded the University Hospital Skin Bank in Louisville, Kentucky.

He is married with four children and loves taking any opportunity to exemplify the phrase “Southern hospitality.”
