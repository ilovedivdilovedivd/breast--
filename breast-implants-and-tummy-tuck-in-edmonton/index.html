Combining Breast Augmentation and Tummy Tuck Surgeries

Combining surgeries such as a tummy tuck and a breast augmentation can be a great option for many patients. Pregnancy can result in a number of changes to a woman’s body and this is most notably seen in both the abdomen and breasts.

Tummy tuck with breast augmentation photo

Skin is stretched, often beyond what the body’s natural elasticity can handle, and then results in a relative sagging of the skin, permanent relaxation of the support structure of the abdominal muscles as well as a loss of breast tissue after breast feeding. As a result, the combination of most breast and abdominal surgeries has been given it’s own name – the “mommy makeover.” The benefits of dual procedures is several fold. Firstly, these surgeries can only be combined if your physician has deemed it safe.

With respect to the surgeries, the recovery time for both procedures occurs simultaneously. This allows for less time off work or away from the care of your children etc. Easing the stress on your family and friends who will help you through the process. An additional benefit is that the cost of the surgery is often less than the two surgeries if done independently. Surgical/OR costs are less for each additional hour in surgery – just think that everything is already open, the staff is there, instruments can be used for both procedures without any problem.

Tummy tuck with breast augmentation images

There are risks, although very small in a healthy patient, with general anesthesia. Thus, only having one anesthetic is better than two – unless there is a medical reason you should not have a longer procedure. Finally, there is a psychological benefit of having the procedures done. I commonly have patients who are anxious about their surgery, and it is nice to be able to have all of this done in one setting. In short, it is a great choice as long as your health permits it, and your doctor can determine this after performing a full exam in a formal consultation. (Vincent P. Marin, MD, San Diego Plastic Surgeon)

Combining these two operations often makes a lot of…

Tummy tuck with breast augmentation image

Combining these two operations often makes a lot of sense. It’s one less trip to the operating room, one less recovery period, and may save you some money in operating room fees, anesthesia, etc. If you are healthy and considering doing both procedures it’s certainly something to talk about with your plastic surgeon. There are some potential drawbacks: The recovery from either of these procedures can be painful, especially for the first day or two, so combining them can be a big hit for some people. It’s a longer operation, and therefore don’t be surprised if your surgeon suggests an overnight stay for monitoring. If you have some potential risk factors for complications (diabetes or other medical conditions) your surgeon might advise you to stage the procedures to be safe. For most 45 year old patients, however, this is a very reasonable option and one we often do in our practice. My advice? Ask to talk to someone who’s had it done, through this message board or one of your surgeon’s previous patients. I think patients can give a unique prospective on the post-op recovery period and pain involved when combining these two. (Scott Thellman, M.D., Lawrence Plastic Surgeon)

Mommy makeovers- discuss safety with your surgeon

As others here have said, this combination of surgeries is commonly performed together. it is important, however, that you discuss with your surgeon the extent of the surgeries and the time he or she anticipates it will take. Personally, I routinely perform straight forward abdominoplasties at the same time as straight forward breast augmentation.

If, however, the abdominoplasty is complicated or involving large amounts of tissue, I may recommend staging the surgeries for safety. One of the major complications of abdominoplasty procedures has classically been blood clots that can form in the legs, and rarely can travel to the lungs and be fatal. most surgeons to everything possible to avoid this issue, including compression of your legs in surgery, and performing the abdominoplasty as the second part of the operation. Some surgeons, like myself, use blood thinners at the time of abdominoplasty surgery to prevent this complication. As blood thinners may increase the possibility of bleeding, operations on other parts of the body at the same time may be problematic in complicated cases. You should discuss these issues with your surgeon, and together make the right decision for you. (Adam David Lowenstein, MD, FACS, Santa Barbara Plastic Surgeon)

What are the components of a Mommy Makeover?

The term Mommy Makeover refers to a combination of procedures designed to return a woman’s body to the pre-pregnancy condition (or better!), by addressing the changes that pregnancy and childbirth bring to (most often) the breasts and abdomen.

Mommy makeover, therefore, can include breast lift, breast augmentation, or breast lift with augmentation, and a tummy tuck, modified tummy tuck, or mini-tummy tuck. Other possible combinations are possible, but I think you get the point.

The most common combination is breast augmentation with a tummy tuck in Edmonton, and as long as you are in good health and an otherwise good candidate for surgery and anesthesia, it does make a lot of sense to combine them. By doing so, you will experience one recovery period rather than two, meaning less time away from family and/or work responsibilities and your preferred activities.

You will usually also save a bit of money, as combining them shortens the overall length of surgery, and by improving the efficiency of your surgeon and the OR team, your costs are also reduced (in most practices). Find a surgeon certified by The American Board of Plastic Surgery and discuss your goals with them. (Armando Soto, MD, FACS, Orlando Plastic Surgeon)

Combing breast implants and abdominoplasty in Edmonton

Each patient is different. The operating time should be limited as already described. You must be a good candidate with no significant medical problems. Most importantly, it is your life and demands on you that are most important. For example, if you have subpectoral implants and abdominoplasty with tightening of the muscles, you are going to be recovering for a while. If you have implants on top of the muscle, your recovery will be a little easier.

You will not be able to lift much, etc. So it depends on who is dependent on you, what your daily activities normally are, etc. Do you have young children? Do you have help at home? Are you a care provider for a parent? How long can you take off from work? All these factors are important. Planning, so your life will not be completely disrupted, is the key.
