Breast augmentation surgery

Surgery and anesthesia

Breast augmentation surgery in Philippines is usually performed in the surgical wards of hospitals, private clinics and surgical centers.

The surgery is performed using general anesthesia, so you will be asleep during the breast job.

Important: Your surgeon should give you a complete and detailed instructions before surgery.

You must to ask a question about which tests and examinations you have to undergo before the breast enlargement.

Also ask what time you should avoid food intakes and any medications and ask about any other recommendations you should follow.

Types of surgical incisions and implant pocket placement

Your surgeon will be the best adviser in a question of pros and cons of different types of incision and access for placement of the implant of recommended for your case. Here are a few facts.

Size of incision: The incision will depend not only on the selected access, but from the size of the implant.

The placement of incision:

Brest Implants By Dr. Marlon O. Lajo

Breast implant emplacement is performed with three (3) types of surgical incisions:

  1. around the nipple (periareolar approach)
  2. in the crease under the breast (inframammary access)
  3. in the armpit (transaxillary access)
The types of incision:

Periareolar approach: this incision well hidden, but due to the fact that it involves the incision through the breast tissue, it is associated with greater probability of difficulty in breast-fed compared to other type of incision. The incision through the breast tissue may be associated with greater probability of sensitivity changes of the breast or nipple.

Philippine Surgery Breast Enlargement At Asian Hospital And Medical Center

The inframammary incision: This incision is the most popular at the moment. Scars are usually hidden in lesser degree than periareolar incision, but difficulties with breastfeeding is rarer.

Transaxillary incision: This incision is not so well hidden, but can be a good option for women who do not want to have scars on the breast.

Placement of implants

During the procedure, breast augmentation implant can be placed either under the breast muscle (submuscular) or on the surface of the muscle, but under the breasts (subglandular). It is important to understand the pros and cons of each option, the implant location. We offer you to get acquainted with the advantages and disadvantages of each option. Your surgeon will offer the approach that will best meet the your features and requirements.

Submuscular placement

Philippines Breast Augmentation Dr. Karlo Capellan

  1. A longer surgery and recovery period.
  2. The pain syndrome is more pronounced during recovery.
  3. Re-operation may be more difficult.
  4. Visualization is facilitated when you do mammography.
  5. The likelihood that the implant will be felt through the skin is less.

Subglandular placement

  1. The operation and recovery period is less prolonged.
  2. Pain is less pronounced during recovery after breast augmentation in the Republic of the Philippines.
  3. Repeated operations easier to perform.
  4. When mammography is deteriorating breast imaging.
  5. The likelihood that the implant will be felt through the skin is high.
