Breast augmentation in Colorado is enlargement of the breast, usually done by inserting an implant beneath the breast to make it look larger.

Placement of the implant: under the mammary gland or under the pectoral muscle?

Breast Augmentation Enlargement Colorado For Larger, More Beautiful Or More Youthful Breasts

Option placement of implants in tissue depends on the amount of cover tissues and a woman plans to give birth and to breastfeed in further. The breast tissue should completely cover the implant, avoiding approximation the shell of the implant to the skin surface. This reduces the risk of capsular contracture. When there is insufficient coating of implants on upper pole may occur so-called “stepped” deformation, which is expressed in sudden, unnatural transition the chest in the upper pole of the breast.

“Wavy” deformation occurs when insufficient cover of soft tissue on the inner edge of the breast. It is expressed in the undulating a relief of the skin on the inner edge of the breast in a vertical position of the body or the when body is bending forward. There are two main intertissue space when installing the implant. The first is subglandular. This space is located between the mammary gland and the pectoral muscle.

The second method is the implant install under the pectoralis major muscle (partialy subpectoral). The pectoralis major muscle covers implants in the upper two-thirds.

Where to make the incision?

There are three main access and one historical placement of the implant. The most optimum access through an incision along the crease under the breast. The length of the incision is no more than 3.5-5 cm.

This is the safest way of placement the implant under the muscle without affecting the very gland. This access is most convenient for the surgeon. It provides a good overview of the surgical field.
