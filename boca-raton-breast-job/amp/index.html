Types access for the implant placement:

Inframammary access. The incision is made in the natural crease under the breast. This allows the implant to install of a fairly large size and any shape. This method is best suited for patients with well-defined crease under the breast, in order to hide the scar.

Periareolar access. The incision is performed a surgeon at the lower edge of the areola. This method complicated by the fact that a cavity for the implant in this incision is more difficult to form. Therefore, there are limitations on the size of the installed implants.

Transaxillary access. The implant is installed through an incision in the armpit. The main advantage is the lack of scar on the breast. There is also transumbilical access (through the navel) and transabdominal (through the abdominal wall). Breast surgery is usually carried out with the first three methods described above, since the use of these techniques is only possible when installing the empty implants, which are subsequently filled with saline solution.

Plastic surgeons are using 3 ways to install implants: installation beneath the pectoralis major muscle, under the gland, or a combination thereof (when one part of the implant is placed under the gland, and the second part – beneath the pectoralis major muscle).

The installation method of the implant under the mammary gland is the easiest in technical terms, and the patient is recovering faster after such surgery. However, this method of increasing breast size has significant drawbacks: with age may occur sagging breasts, as the implant weight falls squarely on breast tissue, edges of the implant may be palpable, the breast does not always look natural. It should be used with appropriate indications.

Also, the implant can be placed beneath the pectoralis major muscle for breast augmentation. The surgeon cuts through the muscle at the bottom and forms the implantation pocket.

Advantages of the method is that in front of the implant lies a layer tissue, and even in lean patients not observed contouring and visualization of the implant.
