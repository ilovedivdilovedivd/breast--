Side effects of breast implants

Remember, deciding on such a complicated operation as boob augmentation, you must be aware of the possible complications of the procedure. These include:

1. Violation of the sensitivity of the nipple and areola. It may manifest itself in the form of a reduction or increase in sensitivity, as well as full anesthesia nipple and areola. Most often this is due to the installation of the implant through an incision around the areola, around the nipple or through the areola. However, a temporary change in sensitivity is mainly due to the stretch of fine tactile nerves for making the pocket where the implant or prosthesis is set during breast augmentation. These effects rarely frighten patients.

2. Purulent wounds around the implant. This “gone wrong” is due to negligence during surgery: it is necessary preoperative administration of broad-spectrum antibiotics, stop bleeding and wash the cavity in the chest with an antiseptic solution and antibiotics during surgery.

Rupture of breast implant

If there was festering, the implant must be removed and to treat the wound with antibiotics.

3. Infection. This risk exists in any surgery. It is necessary to immediately apply antibiotics. If toxic shock symptoms appear (fever, vomiting and diarrhea, fainting or dizziness, rash), is required urgently remove the implant.

4. The accumulation of serous fluid. The emergence of seroma may be due to an excessively large cavity for the implant created by the surgeon, or with absence of wound content outflow – drainage. The participation of the patient is also important. Failure to comply with postoperative regime (Premature increase in physical activity, short-term bandaging and refusal to wear a special bra) increases the likelihood of inflammation.

5. Hematoma. The accumulation of blood is closely associated with suppuration around the implant, and the formation of capsular contracture. Therefore, during the boob enlargement it is important to take into account the indicators of blood clotting. The patient must strictly follow the rules of the recovery period.

6. Necrosis. Necrosis impedes wound healing and leads to scar deformity. Most often it occurs after infection, use of steroids, smoking or chemotherapy, radio-, heat- and cold therapy. With this, resorting to a correction or removal of the implant.

7. Scars after breast augmentation. Formation of keloids or hypertrophic scars is unlikely. However, due to individual tissue reaction on inflicted injuries, they happen during bust enlargement.

8. Changing the shape of the breast. Capsular contracture is presented in the form of a fibrous capsule or scar tissue that is formed around the implant, placed under the breast, and compresses it. Characterized by thickening and modification of forms of breast, unpleasant and painful sensations.

It requires surgical correction. It is also possible wrinkling and thinning of the breast tissue, which leads to chest deformation or atrophy of the breast tissue. This is due to pressure from the implant.

9. The deterioration of the cosmetic effect. Postoperative appearance are not always able to satisfy the client. Some facing such consequences after breast augmentation as its asymmetry, skin folds / wrinkles / stretch marks, wrong-chosen size or shape of the implant, a displacement or palpation of the prosthesis, rough obvious seam. To avoid these complications is necessary to plan the bust enlargement thoroughly.

Rare complications related directly with the implant:

  • displacement / misplacement – erroneous planning of implant placement and cavity size for it, ignoring the specifics of the anatomical features of the structure of the rib cage of the client;
  • deflation / disinflation – saline solution in the prosthesis may be depleted due to diffusion of isotonic sodium chloride solution through the prosthesis shell or through the valve. This gone wrong does not apply to helium implants;

    • breast implants too close together

  • rupture – is due to the thin shell of the implant or of a failed valve, due to unsuccessful breast augmentation surgery (surgical damage of the prosthesis) or the patient’s injuries, as well as due to external pressures (trauma, corset, mammography).
