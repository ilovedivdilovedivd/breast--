Indications for breast augmentation in Belgium

  1. The lack of mammary glands (primary aplasia).
  2. Underdevelopment of the mammary glands (primary hypoplasia).
  3. Involution breast after lactation.
  4. A small degree of ptosis (sagging) of the breast.
  5. Breast reconstruction after a full or partial removal of its tissues.
  6. If you are dissatisfied with the shape and size of breast.

Possible complications

Capsular contracture refers to a group of complications arising after breast augmentation surgery. In this situation, around the installed implant is formed the capsule which compresses and deforms the implant.

This complication sometimes occurs when the volume of the pocket does not match the size of the implant, but in most cases to establish its cause is difficult.
