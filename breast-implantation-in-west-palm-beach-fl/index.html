The operation is performed under the general anesthesia and 1.5 – 2 hours. There are several methods of breast enlargement. The incision and placement the implant will depend from the structure of the breast of the patient and the surgeon’s recommendations.

We offer three options for incisions for placing a breast implant: in the natural crease under the breast (inframammary access), around the areola (periareolar access), in the armpit (transaxillary access). The implant is placed under the pectoral muscle (on the top of the chest), or under the breast (between the muscles of the chest and breast).

1. Inframammary access. An Incision is made on the skin fold under the breast. At the same time, their own breast tissue is minimally damaged and postoperative scar, a length of 4-5 cm, masked skin fold under the breast. Statistics confirm that such access is used in 70-75% of surgeries. The advantage is also the fact that such incision allows placement an implant the anywhere – above the muscle, beneath the muscle and a dual plane breast augmentation (partially under the mammary gland and the muscle). And considering the fact that the surgeon works near to the chest it provides him with optimum visibility.

2. Periareolar access. The incision is performed around the areola – the pigmented area around the nipple so that it is in the zone of transition between dark and light areas of the breast skin, and, thus, perfectly is disguised. The scar is almost impossible to see. But at the same time areola should not be too small size. The disadvantage periareolar type of access is the inability to breastfeed after the operation.

3. Transaxillary access. This method still involves prosthetics mammary glands through the armpit. All actions carried through a small incision in the armpit, where the formed pocket for the implant. The implant is installed through a special endoscopic equipment. The surgeon can see all the tiny blood vessels, to prevent bleeding and anatomical damage, perfectly properly install implants in the breast thanks to this technique. The incision is sutured bioresorbable sutures.

The scar slim is located in an area that hidden from human eyes. The scar is almost imperceptible, but the restoration of the shoulders motor activity proceeds much longer than with other types of incisions.
